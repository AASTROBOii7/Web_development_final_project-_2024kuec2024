Our Academic Curriculum

Aurora Public School follows the **CBSE curriculum**, augmented by an innovative pedagogical approach focused on real-world application, critical thinking, and the development of 21st-century skills. We aim to nurture intellectual curiosity, not just rote memorization.

Stages of Learning

Primary (I–V)

The foundation of knowledge is built on curiosity and play.

  • **Foundational Literacy & Numeracy** (FLN focus)
  • Activity-based learning (A.B.L) and experiential education.
  • Integration of Art and Physical Education into core subjects.
  • Emphasis on good study habits and social etiquette.

Middle (VI–VIII)

Developing conceptual clarity, research skills, and structured thinking.

  • In-depth **Concept Clarity** in Mathematics and Science.
  • Introduction to coding and robotics basics.
  • Mandatory project work and reading programs across all subjects.
  • Pre-vocational skills exposure and language proficiency.

Secondary & Senior Secondary (IX–XII)

Specialized stream selection focusing on career readiness and board preparation.

  • Advanced Board Exam preparation (Classes X & XII).
  • Specialized Streams: **Science, Commerce, and Humanities**.
  • Intensive lab work, research projects, and external guidance programs.
  • Focus on university admissions and competitive exams.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Experiential Learning

We move beyond textbooks to ensure students learn by doing. This includes mandatory science and robotics labs, field trips related to history and geography, and role-playing in language arts.

Skill-Centric Assessment

Assessment goes beyond marks. We evaluate essential skills like critical analysis, collaboration, public speaking, and digital literacy. Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE) guides our feedback.

Labs and Resources

Modern Science Labs

Dedicated, well-equipped labs for Physics, Chemistry, and Biology to facilitate hands-on practical learning from middle school onwards.

Digital Learning Spaces

High-speed internet access, smart classrooms, and a dedicated computer lab for coding, multimedia, and digital literacy training.

Career Guidance

Expert counseling and workshops starting from Class IX to help students align their subject choices with future career paths and university goals.
